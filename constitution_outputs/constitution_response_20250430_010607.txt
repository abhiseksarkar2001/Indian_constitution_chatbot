Specifically, if the land being acquired is under personal cultivation and within the ceiling limit, the government cannot acquire it without providing fair compensation. The compensation must be at least the market value of the property.

If you believe the compensation offered is unfair, you may have the right to challenge the acquisition under the relevant laws and seek appropriate remedies. Additionally, Article 323B provides for the establishment of tribunals to adjudicate disputes related to land reforms and acquisition, which may be a recourse for addressing your concerns.
